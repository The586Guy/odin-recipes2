High-Protein Breakfast Bars

Home

Description

Are you tired of eating the same protein and granola bars from the gas station on your way to work after the gym? Look no further than these simple, healthy, homemade breakfast bars!


Nutrition Information

Servings Per Recipe: 12
Calories: 169

Total Carbohydrates: 21g | Dietary Fiber: 4g | Total Sugars: 8g | Protein: 6g | Total Fat: 8g |
Saturated Fat: 1g | Sodium: 148mg | Calcium: 28mg | Iron: 2mg | Potassium: 264mg


Ingredients

  • Cooking spray
  • 1 1/2 cups quick-cooking oats
  • 1/4 wheat germ
  • 1/4 ground flax seed
  • 2 tablespoons vanilla-flavored protein powder (plant based preferred)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 bananas
  • 1/4 cup peanut butter (name brand of choice)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup natural peanut butter, or to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit (190 degrees Celsius). Line an 8x8-inch baking dish with parchment paper; spray parchment paper with cooking spray of choice.
  2. Stir oats, wheat germ, flax seed, protein powder, cinnamon, and salt together in a bowl. Mash bananas in a separate large bowl and stir 1/4 cup name brand peanut butter, honey, and vanilla extra into bananas, mixing well. Stir dry ingredients into banana mixture and spread into prepared baking dish.
  3. Bake in preheated oven for 20 minutes. Remove and spread natural peanut butter over top.
  4. Continue baking until lightly browned on edges and set (approximately 10 minutes).
  5. Remove from oven and let cool in baking pan. Once cool, move parchment paper and bake onto work surface.
  6. Cut into 12 equal bars, wrap in plastic wrap, and store in refrigerator.
